# FinSight AI Landing Page Strategy

Date: 2026-07-05

## Positioning

FinSight AI should be introduced as a mock/demo financial intelligence platform that combines deterministic scoring, accounting rules, market context, news event intelligence, AI-style explanation, and report workflows.

Primary message:

> AI-powered company intelligence, grounded in financial models.

The landing page must not imply live market feeds, validated fraud detection, real LLM calls, or investment recommendations.

## Target Audience

- Recruiters evaluating product/design/engineering craft.
- Finance, audit, consulting, and investing audiences who expect precision.
- Technical reviewers who need to see the architecture boundary between scoring logic and AI explanation.

## Required Story Arc

1. Hero: what the product does and why it matters.
2. Product preview: show the command center and score system immediately.
3. Intelligence layers: explain how financials, rules, market, news, AI, and reports fit together.
4. Feature bento: show the product breadth without dumping all routes.
5. Scenario simulator: highlight interactivity and explainability.
6. AI analyst: show structured explanation, not generic chat.
7. Responsible intelligence: clarify mock/research limitations.
8. CTA: open dashboard, view companies, try simulator.

## Copy Rules

- Use precise finance/product language.
- Prefer "signals", "requires review", "research platform", "mock data", and "deterministic rules".
- Avoid "guaranteed", "real-time", "buy/sell", "fraud detected", and "LLM-powered" unless a real provider exists.

## Visual Rules

- First viewport should be dark, premium, and spacious.
- Use one sophisticated primary accent with restrained status colors.
- Product preview should resemble a real intelligence command center.
- Avoid generic testimonials unless they are clearly labeled as fictional; this pass removes testimonial claims entirely.

## Implementation Status

Implemented on 2026-07-05:

- Hero headline and CTAs now lead with the product promise and dashboard entry points.
- Product preview now shows portfolio rows, risk trajectory, KPI tiles, and a mock AI analyst memo.
- Intelligence layers explain financial model, red-flag rules, market, news, AI analyst, and reports as separate system layers.
- Scenario and AI analyst sections clarify that deterministic/model-owned scores feed explanation.
- Responsible-use copy now states that the app uses mock company, market, and news data and is not financial advice.
